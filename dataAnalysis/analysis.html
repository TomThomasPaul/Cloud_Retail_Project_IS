Customer Engagement




Looking at the charts we see that the customers generally spend more in December every year which is the holiday season. But we see a spike in March 2021, this is due to the covid-19, which lead to people buying supplies in panic.

Amount spent vs No of children

From this chart we can see that the spending of households has been increasing gradually, but there is a sharp increase in March 2020 which could be due to the lockdowns imposed due to covid. the families are spending more as they are stuck at home.

Amount Spent vs Income Range

We see that the highest amount is spent by customers making 50-74k, followed by people in 35-49k range. People making more than 150K+ are the least spenders, the primary reason for this might be that they might be buying from other premium stores.

Amount Spent vs Age Range

The highest sales is achieved with the age group 45-54, followed by age group 55-64. We see that the younger generation doesnt spend as much at our stores.

Amount Spent vs Homeowner Tye

Majority of the cutomers are homeowners. The Marketing team should look how they can have better customer engaement for this group as it can make a significant impact.

Amount Spent vs Marital Status

The Married group spends slightly more than the Singles at the stores.

Amount Spent vs Brand Type

The national brands sell more as compared to the private brands. This might be due to the trust people have in the national brands.

Potential Growth Opportunities
